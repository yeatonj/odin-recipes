Ginger Molasses Cookies

Photo by Mollie Sivaram on Unsplash

Description

These are my favorite cookies, a loose adaption on a recipe from the Williams Sonoma book Baking. They are a fairly standard ginger molasses cookie with two great characteristics for someone lazy like me: they are easy to make, and they are very tasty! I try to let them chill for longer than the specified one hour and also like to underbake them a little bit, but if you like them a little more crispy you should leave them in the oven for longer.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1.5 teaspoons ground cinanamon
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter at room temperature
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • Granulated sugar

Directions

  1. Sift baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, salt, and flour together into a large bowl.
  2. In a stand mixer (or large bowl), beat the butter and brown sugar until fluffy.
  3. Add molasses and egg to the wet ingredients and mix well.
  4. Slowly add the mixed dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing on low speed until just incorporated. Do not overmix!
  5. Refrigerate, covered, for at least 2 hours.
  6. Set oven to 350°F. Prepare several baking sheets with either parchment paper or baking mats.
  7. Roll dough with hands into 1.5 inch balls, then roll balls in granulated sugar.
  8. Place balls on baking sheets approximately 2 inches apart, then bake for 10-12 minutes.
  9. Let cookies cool for 60 seconds on sheets before moving to wire racks.
