Green Bean Fried Rice

Photo by Jay Nlper on Unsplash

Description

This is a quick and easy fried rice dish. I recommend you use a rice cooker to prepare the rice, but you're welcome to just follow the directions on the rice packaging to do so on the stove if you so choose. You'll want a wok for this recipe; make sure you're cooking everything on high heat!

Many fried rice recipes recommend you cook the rice beforehand and chill it overnight, but I haven't found that completely necessary... Your mileage may vary!

Ingredients

  • Vegetable oil
  • Approximately 1 pound green beans, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 3 cups cooked rice
  • 2-3 Thai chilis, diced into small pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce or oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Coarsely ground pepper

Directions

  1. Heat the wok over high heat and add oil. Pour in the eggs and cook for 20-30 seconds, then flip and cook for another 20-30 seconds. Eggs should be a little soft in the middle but crispy around the edges. Remove into a bowl.
  2. Add the green beans and cook over high heat. Stir constantly; after 2-3 minutes they should be well softened with some charring. Remove them into the same bowl.
  3. Add oil to the wok again. Add the rice and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring as often as possible.
  4. Move rice to the sides and add chilis and garlic. Cook for 20 seconds or so, then mix into rice.
  5. Add the green beans and eggs back to the wok. Break up egg and mix well, cooking for another 30-60 seconds.
  6. Add the fish/oyster sauce and soy sauce and mix well. Add freshly ground pepper to taste, then serve in bowls.
